When a Girl Plays Love Nikki

I played the game Love Nikki for three years. It is a dress-up game with collection and narrative elements. Objectively speaking, the game is well-done in almost all aspects. The gameplay is relaxing. UX is great. Progression is wonderful, new functions unlocked in a reasonable sequence. The narrative is too much fancy for me, but it built up a whole system of world background settings that makes its stories really meaningful to the virtual world. Especially, it has beautiful art and rich contents: nearly 10000 pieces of different cloth (at the time when I uninstalled it in 2017. It could be more now.) With all these great advantages, it is not difficult to understand why it once became the most popular mobile game for girls. Recently (in April 2019), a new game called Shining Nikki was published by the same company. It is still a dress-up game, and it is currently the best 3D dress-up game. But except for its beautiful 3D dresses, Shining Nikki lost most advantages of Love Nikki. The system is too complicated, UX not so friendly, and only has 800+ different cloths. Even so, we girls are still crazy about it.

It's not difficult to understand why girls like dress-up games with collection and narrative elements.
