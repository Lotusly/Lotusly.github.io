A Sound from the Distance

*P.S. This blog only represents personal experiences, personal opinions and feelings. It is recommended not to waste time summarizing it.

Minecraft the game has incredibly high freedom in it. And this freedom is not achieved through complicated designs, but instead because the designer gives the player the right to make his own decisions. Minecraft has very little guidance on player behaviors. Even the tutorial has only basic things such as destroying, placing blocks and building the crafting table. The achievement system (before version 1.11) could have served as a task system, but it does not reward or punish players, which makes it more of a guide. As a sandbox game, the developers hide behind the “simulation world” and puts the player into the center of “creating life”. This is the core spirit of Minecraft and also one of the essential reasons why it attracts so many players. (Minecraft is the second most popular video game in history, only after Tetris.) Countless players have chosen their own lifes in Minecraft: building their homes, collecting items, taking adventures, watching the scenery, raising pets, developing industry and agriculture, designing redstone circuits and music.

This spirit of freedom is expressed everywhere in Minecraft. Minecraft simplifies all world objects into three-dimensional cube models, and then equips the player the main game mechanism: to destroy and place the blocks, which allows the player to freely build the world by simply arranging cubes. Its 3D pixel art style is not only consistent with the world model, but also the abstract and simplified style makes the player focus on the structure of the building, the functions of the blocks, etc., rather than the visual details. Its highly random map generation and creature generation, create highly unique and personalized experiences with simple rules. Its Redstone system gives players the freedom to make electronic devices freely. All aspects of Minecraft work together to support the free spirit and create free experience for players. Among them, Minecraft's background music especially impressed me.

Anyone who has done game background music design work should know some taboos. Here I want to talk about two: the repeated stimulation of the auditory causes the players to fatigue, and the music breaks away from the game world settings and destroys the immersion. I can continuously listen to music for about four hours. It is normal, and some people around me can only do an hour or so. (Of course, there are quite a few people who can listen for more than ten hours.) Every time I play a game for three or four hours, I have to turn off the background music, leaving only the sound effects to provide feedback. In terms of immersion, because background music usually does not belong to the natural sounds in the world setting, more or less players will be stripped out of the game character, feeling "this is a game" instead of "this is a world I really live in." In particular, some background music that is designed to be too radical with colorful personalities,get caught in these two taboos.While Minecraft isn’t. In Minecraft, most of the time there is no background music at all, and the player only hears the sound effects. This makes the world immersive. It also allows players to play continuously without acoustic fatigue so they can focus on the game contents well. On the other hand, Minecraft actually has background music. The background music with soothing rhythm and scattered notes occasionally rings, naturally fades in, and naturally fades out after a while, not noisy at all, becoming a great comfortable experience of the game.

The collection of music CDs in the game is obviously a design that follows the spirit of freedom. What makes me even more interested is that Minecraft's musical atmosphere surprisingly fits its ending (after defeating the dragon) poem. The perspective of the "end poem" is from several higher dimensional creatures. Steve (the protagonist of the game) is observed, admired, and cared about his feelings and experiences. As for the music, Minecraft carefully fades in the background music, and carefully gives players a higher-dimensional perspective. As I said in the last paragraph, background music has the effect of destroying immersion, which is not necessarily a bad thing. The gamers who were immersed in Steve's role-playing, reminded by the background music, changed their perspective to see the story of the world and the protagonist, just as in the "End of Poetry", with appreciation, discussion,curiosity, and care. Because the contents expressed by the music is so much along with the players’ feelings, the players become more concerned about this world and character. The contents expressed by Minecraft's music is undoubtedly suitable for this game.

For a long time, I have always thought that the music of Minecraft is a hidden elaboration. But after reading the background information about the Minecraft music designer C418, I found that the music is the result of casual creation driven by Minecraft’s free spirits. When C418 created music for Minecraft, Minecraft was just a prototype, just a funny model that allowed the player to destroy, pick and place the blocks. C418's feelings about this funny model condensed into the original Minecraft background music, Calm1-3, which is one of the finished Minecraft’s background music. The casual, free and relaxing, quiet and agile things in the music are the concrete manifestation of C418's intuitive feelings about Minecraft's core mechanism and its game spirit. The music is inspired by the core spirit of the game, and turns back adding colors to it, blossoming the emotional resonance inside the players. When the sounds come from the distance, the atmosphere of freedom echoes between the heavens and the earth, the self-exile in the distant world, the resonance of countless souls pursued by self, the spirit expresses itself.

Speaking of this distant music in Minecraft, I think of The Elder Scrolls 5: Skyline. This is probably the game I played for the longest time, and many of its designs have impressed me deeply. As an open world sandbox game, its music is somehow similar to Minecraft. Many times there’s no background music, and when you run above the wilderness in Skyrim province, the distant sky gently echoes the quiet and melodious music piece Far Horizon, touching you softly.

遥远的声音

*注：此篇博客仅代表个人经历，个人观点和感受抒发，建议不要浪费时间归纳主旨。

曾经有一段时间我非常沉迷于玩Minecraft，会不吃不喝地从早玩到晚上。直至今日，当有人问起我对“神作”的定义时，我会这样说：“神作就是在所有的游戏元素上都优秀，而又有那么几点出类拔萃登峰造极。”然后我会想起Minecraft，再补充上一句：“而且所有的游戏元素都切合其核心的精神意志，使整体和谐，浑然天成。”因为我认为Minecraft，这个小神作，就是一件浑然天成的艺术品。

Minecraft的游戏自由度高得不可思议。并且这种高自由度并不是通过繁复的设计来实现的，反而是因为设计者充分给予玩家自己做决定的权利而实现的。Minecraft中对玩家行为的引导极其少，就连tutorial都只有破坏、放置和建造工作台这样基本的项目，成就系统（1.11版前）作为一条可行的游戏主线指引，对玩家也不作奖励或惩罚，而更像是攻略系统。作为一款沙盒游戏，它用心地将开发者隐藏在了“模拟世界”的背后，而将玩家放在了“生活”的中心。这就是Minecraft的核心精神，也是它吸引众多玩家的核心原因之一。（Minecraft是电子游戏史上除了俄罗斯方块之外玩家最多的游戏。）无数的玩家在Minecraft中选择了自己的生活方式：建造自己喜欢的住所，收集物品，进行冒险，观赏风景，豢养宠物，发展工农业，设计红石电路和音乐。

这样的自由思想在Minecraft的众多游戏元素中都得到了体现。它将世界中所有物体都简化为立体方块模型，再给予玩家破坏和放置方块的主游戏机制，使玩家通过排列组合方块就可以自由构建世界。它的立体像素风艺术风格，不但和世界模型完美一致，并且其抽象简化的表达力更使玩家专注于建造物的结构，各方块的职能等本征，而非视觉细节。它的高随机性地图生成、生物生成，也是用极简规则制造高度独特的个性化体验。它的红石系统更是赋予了玩家自由制造电子设备的超能力。Minecraft的方方面面协作着为玩家创造了高度自由的游戏精神和体验。其中，Minecraft的背景音乐尤其令我印象深刻。

凡是做过游戏背景音乐设计的人都应知道诸多大忌，在此我想谈谈其反复刺激听觉导致玩家疲劳，以及脱离游戏世界破坏沉浸感这两点。我大约能连续听音乐四个小时，耐性算是正常的，而我身边的一些人只有一个小时左右的耐性。（当然确实也有不少可以连续听十几小时的高人。）每次当我连续玩一个游戏达到三四小时时，都不得不把背景音乐关掉，只保留音效提供反馈。而在沉浸感方面，背景音乐因为通常不属于世界设定中正常的声音，多少会将玩家剥离出游戏人物，让玩家产生“这是一个游戏”而非“这是一个我真正生活在的世界”的感觉。尤其是一些设计得过于激进和有个性的背景音乐，无时无刻不给玩家以虚拟感受和强听觉刺激。Minecraft则没有这两个问题。在Minecraft中，大部分时候是没有背景音乐的。一片平静之中玩家只听到高低远近错落的动物鸣叫，和自己的脚步声等音效，这就让这个世界显得格外的真实，也能让玩家连续游玩而轻松不疲劳，还能让玩家专注于游戏内容本身。但是呢，它其实也是有背景音乐的。节奏舒缓、音符零散的背景音乐会偶尔响起，自然地淡入，播放一会儿后又自然地淡出，丝毫不干扰玩家，成为游戏的一大舒适体验。

游戏中的唱片收集明显是遵循自由精神的一种设计。此外更让我觉得有趣的一点是，Minecraft的音乐气氛和结局（打败末影龙）之后的《终末之诗》的视角谜之契合。《终末之诗》的视角是几个更高维度空间的存在观察着Steve（游戏主角），议论着，欣赏着，关心着他的感受和体验。而Minecraft在一片安寂中小心淡入的背景音乐，也小心地让玩家获得短暂的更高维度视角。如我在上段中所说，背景音乐有破坏沉浸感的作用，这并不必然是一件坏事。本来沉浸在Steve角色扮演中的游戏玩家，被背景音乐所提醒，而更换了视角去看待这个世界和主角的故事，正像《终末之诗》中所讲的一样，欣赏、议论——关心着那个世界和主角的故事。这种脱离沉浸感，因为音乐中所表达的内容是那样的合乎这个世界，合乎玩家的感受，使得玩家反而更加关心这个拥有了一抹个性的世界和角色。Minecraft的音乐所表达的内容无疑是非常适合这款游戏的。

长期以来，我一直以为Minecraft的音乐音效是精心设计成就的大象无形。但阅读了Minecraft音乐设计师C418的答玩家问和背景资料后，我发现，这音乐是随性创作的结果。C418进行创作时Minecraft仅仅是一个极简的prototype，只是一个能让玩家破坏，拾取和放置方块的滑稽模型。C418对这个滑稽模型的感受凝聚成了最初的Minecraft原声背景音乐，Calm1-3，也即后来应用的Minecraft主背景音乐之一。那音乐中的随性、洒脱、宁静、灵动，正是C418对Minecraft核心机制、游戏精神的直观感受的具象体现。那音乐为游戏的内核所启发，又转而为游戏的内核增色添彩，激发起玩家的情感共鸣。当玩家耳边响起那遥远的声音，在天地间回荡的是自由的气氛，是在遥远异世界自我放逐、自我追求的无数灵魂的共鸣，是一种精神在表达它自身。

说起Minecraft中这种遥远的音乐，我就想起《上古卷轴5：天际》。这大概是我累积游戏时长最长的一部游戏，其中许多设计令我深深折服。作为一款开放世界沙盒游戏，它的音乐竟也与Minecraft有着异曲同工之妙。许多时候并无背景音乐，而往往当我在天际省的无垠旷野之上奔跑的时候，遥远的天空轻轻回荡起那首宁静悠扬的Far Horizon，真乃画龙点睛之笔。
