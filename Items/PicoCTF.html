Our team CryptoKnight from CMU, is developing a game for PicoCTF, the largest hacking competition in the world. Morover, it is a pioneer of competition gamification. Participants will read and answer competition question as a part of gameplay.

The competition aims at performing education in entertainment. It is not only a competition, but also a platform where students are exposed to the attractions of computer science, where they can have fun exploring the computer science world, which they may regard as unattainable before this. It also tries to show people that "hacking" is not necessarily a bad thing. Hacking skills can be used to help poeple, help improve the world in a thousand ways. It is just important to see kindness in it.

Our team CryptoKnight is working tightly with the competition helder Cylab (Security and Privacy Institute, CMU) and competition question provider PPP (Plaid Parliament of Pwning, CMU). In tha past one month (January 2018), we got to understand our objects and limitations through deep conversations in 3 client meetings and 2 technical meetings. We talked and talked to concentrate our bunches of ideas into 3, then 2, then 1. Now we have made a rather detailed game design and job division. We have began working on the development part.

I am (again) a gameplay programmer and sound designer in this project. Also, I participanted a lot in the game design. I am so happy that both my teammates and the clients support my design very much. (See my design here!) So now I'm implementing the nodemap transferring! I'm studying timeline, cinemachine, and trying to implement everything in a resource-saving mode (since performance is important in this project).

It is good to catch dreams; it is even better to chase!
